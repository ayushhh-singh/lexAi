Consumer Protection Act, 2019
=============================

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

Section 1. Short title, extent and commencement  
(1) This Act may be called the Consumer Protection Act, 2019.  
(2) It extends to the whole of India.  
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.

Section 2. Definitions  
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—  
(a) "advertisement" includes any audio or visual publicity, representation or promotion made by means of any medium;  
(b) "consumer" means any person who—  
(i) buys any goods for a consideration;  
(ii) hires or avails of any services for a consideration;  
(iii) uses goods or services with the approval of the buyer;  
(c) "consumer dispute" means a dispute where the person against whom a complaint has been made denies or disputes the allegations contained in the complaint;  
(d) "defect" means any fault, imperfection or shortcoming in the quality, nature or manner of performance of a service;  
(e) "goods" means every kind of movable property;  
(f) "service" means service of any description which is made available to potential users;  
(g) "unfair trade practice" means a trade practice which, for the purpose of promoting the sale, use or supply of goods or services, adopts any unfair method or unfair or deceptive practice.

CHAPTER II — CENTRAL CONSUMER PROTECTION AUTHORITY

Section 10. Constitution of Central Authority  
(1) The Central Government shall, by notification, establish a Central Consumer Protection Authority to promote, protect and enforce the rights of consumers.  
(2) The Central Authority shall consist of a Chairperson and such other members as may be prescribed.

Section 11. Powers of the Central Authority  
The Central Authority shall have the power to—  
(a) conduct investigations into violations of consumer rights;  
(b) initiate class action;  
(c) issue directions to the manufacturers, service providers, and sellers.

CHAPTER III — RIGHTS OF CONSUMERS

Section 12. Rights of consumers  
The consumers shall have the following rights, namely:—  
(a) the right to be protected against the marketing of goods and services which are hazardous to life and property;  
(b) the right to be informed about the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard and price of goods;  
(c) the right to be assured, wherever possible, access to a variety of goods and services at competitive prices;  
(d) the right to seek redressal against unfair trade practices or restrictive trade practices.

CHAPTER IV — REDRESSAL MECHANISM

Section 17. Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission  
(1) The State Government shall establish a Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission for the purpose of providing speedy redressal of consumer disputes.  
(2) The State Commission shall consist of a President and such other members as may be prescribed.

Section 19. Appeal  
Any person aggrieved by an order made by the District Forum or the State Commission may prefer an appeal against such order to the National Commission.

CHAPTER V — OFFENCES AND PENALTIES

Section 69. Penalty for misleading advertisement  
(1) Any person who publishes or causes to be published a misleading advertisement shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years or with fine which may extend to ten lakh rupees or with both.  
(2) If the misleading advertisement is published by a company, every person who was in charge of, and was responsible to the company for the conduct of the business at the time of the commission of the offence shall be deemed to be guilty.

CHAPTER VI — MISCELLANEOUS

Section 75. Power to make rules  
(1) The Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, make rules for carrying out the provisions of this Act.  
(2) Every rule made under this Act shall be laid before each House of Parliament.

Section 76. Repeal and savings  
(1) The Consumer Protection Act, 1986 is hereby repealed.  
(2) Notwithstanding such repeal, any action taken under the repealed Act shall be deemed to have been taken under the corresponding provisions of this Act.
