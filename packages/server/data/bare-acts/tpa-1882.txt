Transfer of Property Act, 1882
==============================

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

Section 1. Short title, extent and commencement  
This Act may be called the Transfer of Property Act, 1882; it extends to the whole of India except the State of Jammu and Kashmir, and it shall come into force on the first day of July, 1882.

CHAPTER II — OF TRANSFERS OF PROPERTY MADE BY ACT OF PARTIES

Section 5. Transfer of property defined  
In the following sections, the expression "transfer of property" means an act by which a living person conveys property, in present or in future, to one or more other living persons, or to himself, or to himself and one or more other living persons.

Section 6. Persons competent to contract  
Every person who is competent to contract and is not disqualified by any law to which he is subject, may transfer property.

Section 7. What may be transferred  
Property of any kind may be transferred, except as otherwise provided by this Act or by any other law for the time being in force.

Section 8. Mode of transfer  
A transfer of property, in the case of a sale, must be made by a registered instrument, and in the case of a mortgage, must be made by a registered instrument.

Section 9. Oral transfer  
A transfer of property may be made orally, unless the law requires a written instrument.

Section 10. Condition restraining alienation  
Where property is transferred subject to a condition restraining alienation, such condition is void.

Section 11. Condition making interest determinable  
Where property is transferred subject to a condition making the interest determinable, such condition is valid.

Section 12. Condition precedent and condition subsequent  
A condition precedent is one which must be fulfilled before the transfer takes effect; a condition subsequent is one which, if it occurs, will defeat the transfer.

Section 13. Transfer for benefit of unborn person  
Where property is transferred for the benefit of an unborn person, the transfer is valid, provided that the unborn person is in existence at the time of the transfer.

Section 14. Property vested in a person  
Property is said to be vested in a person when he is entitled to the immediate possession thereof.

Section 15. Property not vested in a person  
Property is said to be not vested in a person when he is not entitled to the immediate possession thereof.

Section 16. Transfer of property in future  
A transfer of property in future is valid, provided that the transferor has the power to dispose of the property at the time of the transfer.

Section 17. Sale  
A sale is a transfer of ownership in exchange for a price paid or promised or part-paid and part-promised.

Section 18. Mortgage  
A mortgage is the transfer of an interest in specific immovable property for the purpose of securing the repayment of a loan.

Section 19. Lease  
A lease of immovable property is a transfer of the right to enjoy such property, made for a certain time, express or implied, in consideration of a price paid or promised.

Section 20. Exchange  
An exchange is a transfer of ownership in one property for the ownership of another property.

Section 21. Gift  
A gift is the transfer of certain existing movable or immovable property made voluntarily and without consideration.

Section 22. Actionable claim  
An actionable claim is a claim to any debt, or any beneficial interest in movable property, which is not secured by a mortgage or hypothecation.

CHAPTER III — OF TRANSFERS OF PROPERTY BY OPERATION OF LAW

Section 23. Transfer by operation of law  
A transfer of property may also be made by operation of law, such as in the case of inheritance or succession.

CHAPTER IV — OF THE RIGHTS AND LIABILITIES OF TRANSFEREES AND TRANSFERRERS

Section 30. Rights of transferee  
A transferee of property has the right to enjoy the property and to receive the rents and profits thereof.

Section 31. Liabilities of transferee  
A transferee is liable to the transferor for any loss or damage caused by his negligence or default.

Section 32. Rights of transferor  
A transferor has the right to receive the consideration for the transfer and to enforce the terms of the transfer.

Section 33. Liabilities of transferor  
A transferor is liable for any misrepresentation or fraud in relation to the transfer.

CHAPTER V — OF THE REGISTRATION OF TRANSFERS

Section 47. Registration of documents  
No document of transfer shall be deemed to be valid unless it is registered in accordance with the provisions of the Registration Act, 1908.

CHAPTER VI — OF MISCELLANEOUS

Section 53. Fraudulent transfers  
Every transfer of property made with intent to defeat or delay the creditors of the transferor shall be voidable at the instance of any creditor so defeated or delayed.

Section 54. Sale how made  
A sale of immovable property is made by a registered instrument, and the transfer is complete when the instrument is executed.

Section 55. Rights and liabilities of lessor and lessee  
In the absence of a contract to the contrary, the lessor and lessee shall have the rights and liabilities as provided in this section.

Section 56. Doctrine of part performance  
Where a contract for the transfer of property is made and the transferee has taken possession of the property, the transfer shall not be denied on the ground of non-registration.

Section 57. Transfer of property by a person not having title  
A transfer of property made by a person who is not the owner shall be void, unless the transferee is a bona fide purchaser for value without notice of the defect of title.

This concludes the key sections of the Transfer of Property Act, 1882.
