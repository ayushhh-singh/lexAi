**CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY**

**Section 1. Short title, extent, and commencement**  
This Act may be called the Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023. It extends to the whole of India and shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.

**Section 2. Definitions**  
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:  
(a) "Accused" means a person against whom a complaint has been made or an FIR has been registered;  
(b) "Victim" means a person who suffers harm, including physical or mental injury, as a result of the commission of an offence;  
(c) "Police" means the police force constituted under any law for the time being in force.

---

**CHAPTER II — OFFENCES AND PENALTIES**

**Section 3. Offences against the State**  
Whoever commits an act that is prejudicial to the sovereignty, integrity, or security of India shall be punished with imprisonment for life or with imprisonment for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.

**Section 4. Offences against public tranquility**  
Whoever commits an act that causes public disturbance or riot shall be punished with imprisonment which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

**Section 5. Punishment for murder**  
Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine.

**Section 6. Punishment for culpable homicide**  
Whoever commits culpable homicide not amounting to murder shall be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall also be liable to fine.

---

**CHAPTER III — INVESTIGATION AND TRIAL**

**Section 7. Power to arrest without warrant**  
Any police officer may arrest without warrant any person who, in his view, has committed an offence punishable under this Act.

**Section 8. Procedure for investigation**  
Every investigation under this Act shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973.

**Section 9. Trial of offences**  
All offences under this Act shall be triable in a Court of Sessions or a Magistrate’s Court as specified in the schedule.

---

**CHAPTER IV — RIGHTS OF VICTIMS AND ACCUSED**

**Section 10. Rights of victims**  
Victims of offences under this Act shall have the right to:  
(a) receive information about the progress of the investigation;  
(b) be treated with dignity and respect;  
(c) receive compensation as per the provisions of this Act.

**Section 11. Rights of accused**  
The accused shall have the right to:  
(a) a fair trial;  
(b) legal representation;  
(c) be presumed innocent until proven guilty.

---

**CHAPTER V — MISCELLANEOUS**

**Section 12. Power to make rules**  
The Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, make rules for carrying out the provisions of this Act.

**Section 13. Repeal and savings**  
The provisions of any law in force immediately before the commencement of this Act shall stand repealed, but such repeal shall not affect any right, privilege, obligation, or liability acquired, accrued, or incurred under such law.

**Section 14. Act to override other laws**  
The provisions of this Act shall have effect notwithstanding anything inconsistent therewith contained in any other law for the time being in force.
