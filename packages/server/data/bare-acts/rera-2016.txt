Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016
==================================================

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

Section 1. Short title, extent and commencement
(1) This Act may be called the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016.
(2) It extends to the whole of India except the State of Jammu and Kashmir.
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.

Section 2. Definitions
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
(a) "agent" means a person who acts on behalf of another person in the sale, purchase or lease of a plot, apartment or building;
(b) "allottee" means a person to whom a plot, apartment or building has been allotted;
(c) "builder" means a person who constructs or causes to be constructed a building;
(d) "real estate project" means a project that involves the development of land into plots, apartments or buildings.

CHAPTER II — REGULATION OF REAL ESTATE SECTOR

Section 3. Applicability
The provisions of this Act shall apply to all real estate projects and to the real estate agents.

Section 4. Registration of real estate project
(1) It shall be mandatory for the promoter to register the real estate project with the Authority.
(2) The application for registration shall be made in such form and manner as may be prescribed.

Section 5. Grant of registration
(1) The Authority shall, on receipt of an application under section 4, grant registration to the real estate project.
(2) The Authority may refuse to grant registration if the application is not complete or if the applicant has been convicted of any offence.

Section 6. Revocation of registration
(1) The Authority may revoke the registration of a real estate project if the promoter fails to comply with the provisions of this Act.
(2) The promoter shall be given an opportunity of being heard before revocation.

CHAPTER III — RIGHTS AND DUTIES OF PROMOTERS AND ALLOTTEES

Section 11. Obligations of promoters
The promoter shall be responsible for the completion and handover of the project in accordance with the terms of the agreement for sale.

Section 12. Obligations of allottee
The allottee shall make payment for the plot, apartment or building in accordance with the terms of the agreement for sale.

Section 13. Title of the promoter
The promoter shall ensure that he has a legal title to the land and shall not create any encumbrance on the land.

CHAPTER IV — ESTABLISHMENT OF REAL ESTATE REGULATORY AUTHORITY

Section 20. Establishment of Authority
(1) The Central Government shall, by notification, establish an Authority to be known as the Real Estate Regulatory Authority.
(2) The Authority shall consist of a Chairperson and such other members as may be prescribed.

Section 21. Functions of Authority
The functions of the Authority shall include—
(a) promoting the real estate sector;
(b) protecting the interests of consumers;
(c) ensuring compliance with the provisions of this Act.

CHAPTER V — PENALTIES AND ADJUDICATION

Section 63. Penalty for contravention
(1) Any promoter who fails to comply with the provisions of this Act shall be liable to a penalty which may extend to ten percent of the estimated cost of the real estate project.
(2) Any person who contravenes any provision of this Act shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years.

Section 64. Offences by companies
(1) If a company commits an offence under this Act, every person who was in charge of and was responsible for the conduct of the business of the company shall be deemed to be guilty of the offence.

CHAPTER VI — MISCELLANEOUS

Section 84. Power to make rules
The Central Government may, by notification, make rules for carrying out the provisions of this Act.

Section 85. Power to make regulations
The Authority may, with the previous approval of the Central Government, make regulations for the purpose of carrying out the provisions of this Act.

Section 86. Act to have overriding effect
The provisions of this Act shall have effect notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in any other law for the time being in force.

Section 87. Application of other laws
The provisions of this Act shall be in addition to and not in derogation of the provisions of any other law for the time being in force.
