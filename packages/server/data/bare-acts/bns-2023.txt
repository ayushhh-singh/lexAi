Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023
=============================

**Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (BNS)**

---

**CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY**

**Section 1. Short title, extent, and commencement**  
(1) This Act may be called the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023.  
(2) It extends to the whole of India.  
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.

**Section 2. Definitions**  
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:  
(a) "Act" means this Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023;  
(b) "Court" means a court established under this Act;  
(c) "Offence" means any act or omission made punishable by this Act.

---

**CHAPTER II — GENERAL PRINCIPLES OF CRIMINAL LAW**

**Section 3. Punishment of offences**  
(1) Every offence shall be punishable in accordance with the provisions of this Act.  
(2) The punishment shall be proportionate to the gravity of the offence.

**Section 4. Offences committed by several persons**  
When an offence is committed by several persons, each of them shall be liable to punishment as if he had committed the offence alone.

**Section 5. Abetment**  
A person abets an offence who, in any manner, aids, abets, or instigates another to commit it.

---

**CHAPTER III — COGNIZABLE AND NON-COGNIZABLE OFFENCES**

**Section 6. Cognizable offences**  
(1) Cognizable offences are those for which a police officer may arrest without a warrant.  
(2) The list of cognizable offences shall be prescribed by the Central Government.

**Section 7. Non-cognizable offences**  
(1) Non-cognizable offences are those for which a police officer cannot arrest without a warrant.  
(2) The list of non-cognizable offences shall be prescribed by the Central Government.

---

**CHAPTER IV — BAIL AND ARREST**

**Section 8. Bailable and non-bailable offences**  
(1) An offence shall be bailable if it is so declared by this Act.  
(2) All other offences shall be non-bailable.

**Section 9. Arrest without warrant**  
A police officer may arrest without a warrant any person who, in his view, is committing a cognizable offence.

---

**CHAPTER V — TRIAL OF OFFENCES**

**Section 10. Trial of offences**  
(1) All offences under this Act shall be tried in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.  
(2) The trial shall be conducted in a summary manner for minor offences.

**Section 11. Rights of accused**  
(1) Every person accused of an offence shall be presumed innocent until proven guilty.  
(2) The accused shall have the right to legal representation.

---

**CHAPTER VI — SENTENCES**

**Section 12. Types of sentences**  
(1) The following types of sentences may be imposed:  
(a) Death penalty;  
(b) Imprisonment for life;  
(c) Imprisonment for a term;  
(d) Fine.

**Section 13. Sentencing principles**  
In determining the sentence, the court shall consider the nature of the offence, the circumstances of the offender, and the impact on the victim.

---

**CHAPTER VII — APPEALS AND REVISIONS**

**Section 14. Appeals**  
(1) Any person convicted of an offence may appeal to the higher court within the prescribed time.  
(2) The appeal shall be heard in accordance with the provisions of this Act.

**Section 15. Revision**  
The High Court may call for the record of any case and may revise any order passed by a subordinate court.

---

**CHAPTER VIII — MISCELLANEOUS**

**Section 16. Power to make rules**  
The Central Government may make rules for carrying out the provisions of this Act.

**Section 17. Repeal and savings**  
(1) The existing laws relating to criminal offences are hereby repealed.  
(2) However, any proceeding initiated under the repealed laws shall continue until concluded.
