---

CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

Section 1. Short title, extent, and commencement  
(1) This Act may be called the Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023.  
(2) It extends to the whole of India.  
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.

Section 2. Definitions  
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:  
(a) "Court" means a civil or criminal court;  
(b) "Evidence" includes all statements which the Court permits or requires to be made before it by witnesses, and all documents produced for the inspection of the Court;  
(c) "Witness" includes every person whose evidence is relevant to the matter in issue.

---

CHAPTER II — RELEVANCY OF FACTS

Section 5. Evidence may be given of facts in issue and relevant facts  
Evidence may be given of facts in issue and of such other facts as are hereinafter declared to be relevant, and of no others.

Section 6. Relevancy of facts forming part of the same transaction  
Facts which, though not in issue, are so connected with the facts in issue as to form part of the same transaction, are relevant.

Section 7. Relevancy of facts showing existence of state of mind, etc.  
Facts showing the existence of any state of mind, or of any intention, or of any fact in issue, are relevant.

---

CHAPTER III — ORAL AND DOCUMENTARY EVIDENCE

Section 17. "Document" defined  
For the purposes of this Act, "document" includes any matter expressed or described upon any substance by means of letters, figures, or marks.

Section 18. Admission  
An admission is a statement, oral or documentary, which suggests any inference as to any fact in issue or relevant fact.

Section 19. Confession  
A confession is an admission made at any time by a person charged with a crime, stating or suggesting the inference that he committed that crime.

---

CHAPTER IV — WITNESSES

Section 26. Competency of witnesses  
All persons shall be competent to testify unless the Court sees fit to disqualify them.

Section 27. Examination of witnesses  
Witnesses shall be examined in the following order:  
(a) Examination-in-chief;  
(b) Cross-examination;  
(c) Re-examination.

Section 29. Privileged communications  
No one shall be compelled to disclose any communication made to him in professional confidence by a client.

---

CHAPTER V — PRESUMPTIONS

Section 113. Presumption as to absence of consent  
When a person is found dead under circumstances that raise suspicion of murder, the Court may presume that the person was murdered and that the accused is guilty unless proven otherwise.

Section 114. Presumption as to documents  
The Court may presume the existence of certain facts, unless the contrary is proved.

---

CHAPTER VI — MISCELLANEOUS

Section 136. Power of the Court to decide admissibility of evidence  
The Court shall decide whether evidence is admissible or not.

Section 137. Order of production and examination of witnesses  
The order of production and examination of witnesses shall be determined by the Court.

Section 139. Production of documents  
The Court may, at any stage of the proceedings, order the production of any document.
